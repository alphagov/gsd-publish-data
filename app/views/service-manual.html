Publish data Publishing to Government Service Data

Published by:
Government Service Data team
Last updated:
31 January 2017

Give feedback about this page

Government Service Data is a reporting tool that lets government services publish data about their transactions. Its main users are civil servants working to transform services.

Government Service Data is designed to help these users understand:

  • the demands faced by services - the volume of transactions they receive across different channels, and how often each channel is used and why
  • how well services are meeting their users' needs
  • how far the service has been digitised

Which services should publish data

You only need to publish to Government Service Data if your service is transactional.

A transactional service is one that allows the user to interact with government. Services that allow the user to do or change something (rather than simply providing content) are transactional. A user using this type of service successfully results in a transaction.

Here are some examples of transactional services:

  • Making a claim
  • Applying for a licence
  • Buying a product
  • Booking a service
  • Applying for a benefit
  • Paying a fine or a tax bill

What to publish

The Government Service Data team has developed a new set of KPIs designed to work for service transformation.

The KPIs are:

Total number of transactions received

This KPI helps users gauge the overall demand for the service.

Total number of transactions received, split by channel

This KPI helps users see which channels are used the most.

Channels include:

  • online
  • phone
  • paper form
  • face-to-face
  • other

Number of transactions ending in an outcome

This KPI gives users an indication of how well the service is meeting users’ needs.

Number of transactions ending in the user’s intended outcome

This KPI gives users more information about how well the service is meeting users’ needs.

Total number of phone calls received

This KPI shows how much the service's contact centre is used.

Number of phone calls received, split by reasons for calling

This KPI shows why calls are made and gives an indication of how the number of calls can be reduced.

Reasons include:

  • to get information
  • to chase progress
  • to challenge a decision
  • Other

When to publish

Services should publish data on a monthly basis, two months in arrears. For example, data from February would be published in April.

How to publish

Sign up to use the data publishing service. You'll receive an email each month reminding you to publish your data.
